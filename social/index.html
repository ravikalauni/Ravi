MY THOUGHTS ON SOCIAL MEDIA POSTING

IN TODAY'S DIGITAL AGE, IT FEELS LIKE PEOPLE HAVE DEVELOPED A HABIT OF SHARING EVERY MINOR DETAIL OF THEIR LIVES ONLINE. POSTS LIKE "SUNDAY SPECIAL," "OUT TO THE PARK," OR GENERIC CAPTIONS ABOUT "HAPPY HOMENTS" HAVE BECOME INCREDIBLY COMMON. AND TO BE HONEST, IT COMES ACROSS AS A LITTLE EXCESSIVE.

SOCIAL MEDIA, AT ITS BEST, SHOULD BE A PLACE TO SHARE MEANINGFUL EXPERIENCES—MOMENTS THAT GENUINELY HOLD VALUE, MEMORIES THAT YOU'LL LOOK BACK ON AND REMEMBER WITH A SENSE OF PURPOSE OR EMOTION. NOT EVERY MEAL OR OUTING NEEDS AN AUDIENCE.

WHEN WE OVERSHARE, WE UNINTENTIONALLY BEGIN TO BULITE THE SIGNIFICANCE OF TRULY SPECIAL MOMENTS. IT'S NOT JUST ABOUT WHAT YOU POST; IT'S ALSO ABOUT WHAT IT SAYS ABOUT YOU. YOUR ONLINE PRESENCE REFLECTS YOUR PERSONALITY, AND IF IT STARTS TO LOOK PERFORMATIVE OR OVERLY CURATED, IT CAN GIVE PEOPLE THE IMPRESSION THAT YOU'RE MORE FOCUSED ON APPEARANCES THAN AUTHENTICITY.

IN FACT, CONSTANTLY TRYING TO SHOWCLOSE A HAPPY OR PERFECT LIFE ONLINE CAN SOMETIMES DO THE OPPOSITE OF WHAT'S INTENDED. IT CAN MAKE OTHERS QUESTION WHETHER THAT HAPPINESS IS GENUINE OR JUST A FAÇADE FOR LINES AND VALIDATION. SO INSTEAD OF SHARING EVERYTHING, I THINK IT'S MORE VALUABLE TO POST WITH INTENTION—ONLY WHEN IT REALLY MEANS SOMETHING.

AFTER ALL, NOT EVERYTHING NEEDS TO BE PUBLIC TO BE MEANINGFUL.
