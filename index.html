This pencil sketch of the Mona Lisa was created over a span of several months, from December 10, 2024, to March 23, 2024. Although the sketch remains incomplete, I have decided to accept it as a completed work. Throughout the process, I aimed to capture the essence, emotion, and timeless beauty of Leonardo da Vinci's masterpiece, rather than focusing solely on perfecting every detail.

As I worked on this piece, I encountered moments of challenge and reflection, which helped me understand the depth and complexity behind the original painting. Every line, shade, and unfinished space tells a part of my creative journey. Instead of striving for flawlessness, I embraced the imperfections and allowed the sketch to reflect the natural flow of my hand and thoughts at the time.

This artwork represents more than just a technical exercise; it symbolizes my growth as an artist, my patience, and my willingness to accept that sometimes, beauty lies within the unfinished and the imperfect. Through this sketch, I hope to convey a sense of respect for the original masterpiece while also showcasing my personal interpretation and artistic process.

This drawing of a cow baby, the national animal of Nepal, was created in 2023. The contrast between the bold, colorful background and the realistic portrayal of the calf emphasizes the simplicity and purity of the subject. While the calf remains the focal point, the background provides an interesting dynamic that enhances the overall composition. This drawing represents my ongoing exploration of blending traditional techniques with modern styles, as well as my deep respect for the cultural symbolism tied to the cow in Nepal. Though simple in design, this piece holds personal significance and reflects my growth as an artist.
